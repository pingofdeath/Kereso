László Lovász.

László Lovász (born March 9, 1948 in Budapest, Hungary) is a mathematician, best known for his work in combinatorics, for which he was awarded the Wolf Prize and the Knuth Prize in 1999.

Lovász received his Ph.D. in 1970 at Eötvös Loránd University. His advisor was Tibor Gallai, professor at the University of Szeged.

Lovász was a professor at Yale University during the 1990s and was a collaborative member of the Microsoft Research Center until 2006. Now he has returned to Eötvös Loránd University, Budapest, where he is the director of the Mathematical Institute.

He has served as president of the International Mathematical Union since January 1, 2007[1].
